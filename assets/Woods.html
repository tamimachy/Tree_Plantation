Woods of Tree

Benefit of wood in construction

Wood carries its thermal properties ,which give it an advantage in terms of its resistance to high temperature addition the heat conductivity wood is relatively low in comparison to materials such as aluminium ,marble, steel or glass .It can observe sound and echo, and it is a favourite material of choice for the construction of structures .Wood is resistant to electrical currents , making it optimal material for electrical insulation .Wood is a prominent material in the furniture industry .Furniture made of wood is one of the few things, in the world that all people can own. Nowadays along with furniture wood is also used in wood veneer, wood flooring and cladding. There are different kinds of wood available in nature. Different kinds of wood have different properties, colour, texture and strength .Here we have given brief introduction on different kinds of wood used in the construction industry, which a homeowner must know.

There are two types of wood.

1. Softwood

2. Hardwood

Softwood

Softwood comes from the coniferous tree .These trees are also known as evergreen tree are and levees of these tree do not fall till the new one is grown .Softwood usually grown in gold climates. Coniferous tree grows fast so they are less expensive as compared to hardwood. There are different types of softwood which are given below.

1. Pine Wood

Pine is a soft, white or light-yellow wood which is light in weight, straight grained. It resists swelling and warping. Pine is very easy to sharp and stain. Pine decease easily, when it come in contact with soil. It is a found in India, Western, Himalayas, Assam and Nagpur.

2. Cedar Wood

Cedar is knotty softwood which has a red-brown colour with light lines. Cedar is soft as compared to other softwood. Its texture is uniform and it is highly resistant to decay and insects. They are aromatic in nature. It is found in Kashmir and Assam.

3. Fri Wood

It is often referred to as Douglas Fri. Fri has straight grain and has reddish brown colour. It is uniform in texture and non-resinous. It is low resistant to decay. Fri is hardwood as compared to other softwoods. It cannot be stained easily hence generally it is used in raw from or as painted surface. Fri is found in North and Central America, North Africa, Europe and Asia. In India, it is found in Himachal Pradesh.

Hardwood

Hardwood comes from deciduous tree that have broad leaves, produce fruit or nut, and are generally, inactive in the winter. Hardwood has denser as compared to softwoods. Different types of hardwood can be described as follows.

Teak Tree

Teak Wood
(1) Teak Wood

Teakwood is the most popular types of hardwood. It is yellow to dark brown in colour which is extremely heavy, strong, durable, weather resistant, and warp resistant and does not decay. Often strongly figured, teak may show straight grain pattern. The natural oils in teak make them termite resistant and pest resistant. It does not burn easily. Teak is most expensive as compared to other types of wood. It is generally found in central and Sothern India.

Rose Tree

Rose Wood
(2) Rose Wood

Rosewood is very hardwood and has a dark reddish brown colour. It has unique fragrance. It is hard to work and take a high polish. Rosewood is classified as Brazilian rosewood, East India rosewood, Honduras rosewood, Amazon rosewood etc. East India rosewood is also known Hindi. Rosewood is considered very valuable. In India, It is generally found mostly in Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Mysore, Bengal, Assam, Tamil Nadu and Orissa.

Maple Tree

Maple Wood
(3) Maple Wood

Maple wood has a fine texture with great strength. It is so hard and resistant to shocks that it is often used for a pathway. Maple is resistant to splitting and is very durable. It is also very easy clean maple wood. Maple is found in Asia, North America, North Africa, Europe and Canada. In the India you can find this tree in oozy and other hilly regions.
